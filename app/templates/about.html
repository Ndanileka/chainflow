Understanding the Mechanics of Collapse

ChainFlow is an interactive educational tool designed to visualize the mathematical certainty behind unsustainable financial systems.

The Math Never Lies

Whether it is a classic Ponzi scheme from the 1920s, a multi-level marketing (MLM) structure from the 1990s, or a decentralized yield farm from the 2020s, the fundamental mathematics remain identical: early participants are paid using the capital of later participants.

Because the promised returns exceed the actual value generated (usually zero), the system requires an exponentially growing base of new recruits. Once the required influx of capital exceeds the available local population or market saturation point, a liquidity crisis triggers a sudden and catastrophic collapse.

Yield Drip vs. Lump Sum

Our simulation engine supports two primary models of wealth extraction:

  • Lump Sum Maturity: Participants lock their capital for a set period and then withdraw their principal and profit all at once. This creates massive, unpredictable drains on the cash pool (e.g., Madoff, Blessing Loom).
  • Yield Drip: The scheme promises an APY (e.g., 20%) and pays it out incrementally while holding the principal hostage. This slows the drain on the cash pool, allowing the illusion of solvency to persist for much longer before the inevitable bank run (e.g., Anchor Protocol).
